Liquidity protocol on Radix DLT

Discover the Power of Flash Loans!

Sundae Liquidity Protocol currently offers flash loans as its main feature. Flash loans are loans that are issued and returned within a single (same) transaction. This page provides a function to build and execute a flash loan. Before you get started it is advised to read the documentation for borrowers on Sundae platform.

Background on flash loans

Flash loans are made possible due to one of Radix's key features: atomic composability. Atomic composability in Radix (and crypto in general) is the ability to combine multiple transactions into a single atomic transaction, allowing for complex transactions to be executed in a single step. This enables flash loans because it allows users to borrow assets and repay the loan in the same transaction, without the need for collateral or credit checks, as the entire transaction is executed atomically. Read more on atomic composability here.

Here is how to get started:

[1] Connect wallet

Connect your wallet to Sundae's dApp. Install the Radix wallet on your phone and browser extension on desktop, in case you haven't yet. Thereafter you can connect to Sundae's dApp by using the top right button. This opens a prompt on the Radix wallet on your phone asking you to accept the connection.

[2] Build flash loan

The process of constructing and executing a flash loan can be divided into three steps, outlined as follows:

[2.1] Get a flash loan

Call the flash loan function on the smart contract, passing in the amount of funds you want to borrow. The smart contract will execute the loan and send the funds to your wallet.

[2.2] Use borrowed funds

Use the borrowed funds to execute your desired transaction or strategy. Flash loans are typically used to take advantage of arbitrage opportunities or to execute complex trading strategies.

[2.3] Return borrowed funds

Return the borrowed funds including interest rate to the smart contract before the end of the loan period, along with the small interest fee. Failure to return the funds and fee will result in the transaction being reverted and the borrower losing the borrowed funds.
