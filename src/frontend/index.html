Stake and Earn Interest, or Borrow

Sundae is a decentralized liquidity market protocol built on Radix DLT, where users can participate as borrowers or suppliers. The protocol offers lending products for borrowers, and liquidity solutions for suppliers. Built on Radix DLT, the protocol offers transparency, security, and innovative solutions for traders and investors. This allows users to enjoy fast and efficient transaction processing, low fees, and transparent terms.

This is how it works:

[1] Stake XRD

The first step into becoming a liquidity provider, is to stake XRD at Sundae's validator node. In return you will receive 'Liquid staking units' (LSU's) - which serve as proof of your stake.

The validator node claims the staking rewards on your behalf, and deposits them automatically into the liquidity pool.

[2] Deposit LSU's

Depositing these LSU's into the liquidity pool will register you as a liquidity supplier. In return, a unique NFT will be deposited to your wallet - which serves as proof of your supply. Using this NFT, the protocol keeps track of your entitled amount of LSU and (staking and interest) earnings

[3] Earn Interest

The by you provided liquidity is used to issue lending products (flash loans) to borrowers on Sundae. The interest fees that are collected are allocated proportionally to you.

[4] Claim Resources

Finally, once you decide to withdraw your liquidity, your unique NFT allows you to instantly and at any time claim:

  • LSU's that you have deposited to the protocol
  • Staking rewards that you are entitled to
  • Interest earned by the liquidity pool

Subsequently these LSU's allow you to unstake and return your staked XRD.

Access liquidity instantly; anywhere!

Borrow at Sundae's:

Sundae Liquidity Protocol currently offers flash loans as its main feature. Flash loans enable you to take out loans without collateral that are executed within a single transaction. Liquidity can be accessed instantly and at low interest rates, to make profitable trades or take advantage of market inefficiencies. This allows anyone to participate in DeFi markets in a more accessible and cost-effective way. Overall, Sundae's flash loan feature offers a powerful tool for financial innovation and market participation.

Version 1 - 13 August 2023

Sundae's first iteration of Sundae liquidity protocol will be released shortly after the Radix Babylon launch. This release will consist of the following features:

  • Validator node

    • A validator node will be deployed that collects staking rewards, and deposits it in a liquidity pool on behalf of the staker.

  • Liquidity pool

    • The first liquidity pool will collect liquidity via staking rewards. A novel approach to provide liquidity, that enables liquidity providers to leverage on their staking rewards. This offers unique benefits, especially at Babylon launch when the staking yield (APY) will be relatively high.

  • Flash loan

    • The first offered lending product will be flash loans. Flash loans offer instant liquidity and the ability to lower spreads across exchanges. Bringing these features into the Radix ecosystem at an early stage will contribute to Radix's succes and ability to grow.

Version 1+

In the remainder of 2023, after the launch of Sundae Version 1, Sundae will prioritize the following initiatives:

  • User adoption

    • By utilizing social media channels (Twitter, Telegram, Youtube, etc.) and improving the user experience.

  • Increased liquidity

    • This will be achieved by promoting Sundae's validator node to XRD holders.

Version 2, ... , n

Subsequent iterations, taking place in 2024, will expand the protocol on the following aspects (in no particular order):

  • Sundae governance/utility token
  • Lending products
  • Supported (stable) tokens
  • Liquidity pools and methods for providing liquidity

Decentralized; Transparent; Secure

Sundae liquidity protocol is non-custodial. Liquidity pools and lending products are governed by the laws of smart contracts. Sundae's smart contracts are designed so that only the rightful owner can claim their portion of the pool's liquidity.
